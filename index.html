Introduction

So you are an artist, a designer, a conceptual artist/designer, an artistic researcher/writer, a critical designer/curator or a student at a critical master department of an artsy conceptual design school, and you’re looking for a way to visualize your research in a way that is both critical and educative but also conceptually interesting, somewhat entertaining, and definitely aesthetically pleasing. Your video skills are too average to make your work match your refined video-taste, but you still really want to make a video... Join the club, you’re an amateur like us.

This is great. To be an amateur is to be a lover of something. An amateur filmmaker is a passionate maker not limited by rules and expectations, free to experiment and always eager to learn more.

Like us, you might have various video-ideas scribbled down in your notebook already. But meanwhile 1,300,000,000.3 hours of video are uploaded to YouTube every minute, and about 5 billion videos are watched on Youtube every single day. Among these viewers are other aspiring videomakers, watching tutorials on how to edit videos, video essays about video essays, unboxing videos of video-equipment, and uploading their own amateur videos. We have no excuse & no time to lose.

YouTube was basically built on the backs of amateur videomakers. But today, with equipment getting more widely available, tutorials on any film-related skill only being one mouse click and 2 ads away. As YouTubers are now making money from their vids, able to afford better lighting & camera equipment, and even calling YouTube a 'full time job', the lines between amateur and professional have completely blurred. Creators are increasingly tempted to cater to The Algorithm instead of their inner lover. Where are the amateurs? (Where is the love?)

So while the entire world is busy becoming professional videomakers, let us embrace our inner amateur. During this 3-day workshop we will learn ways to sophisticatedly put together Awesome Videos, while creatively using the possibilities and limits of our computer screens! No need for studios, lighting or cameras; all we need is a laptop and an internet connection.

Using found footage, screen recording and many other interface-based forms of video creation, stories can be told in interesting, entertaining, even poetic ways. We will use various methods found in desktop films, and look at multiple examples of these and other disruptive filmmaking examples. Using YouTube as our library, our school and ultimately our cinema, the goal is to set up our own channel and fill it with experimental content that will inspire all of you in your future video-journeys.
